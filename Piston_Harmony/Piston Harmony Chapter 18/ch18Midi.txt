ex1 — V9 major → I major, V9 minor → I major, V9 minor → i minor. The minor ninth (Ab) gives a darker, more urgent quality; it resolves into both major and minor. The major ninth (A) is brighter and restricted to major tonic resolution.
ex2 — spacing demonstration: forbidden voicing (ninth below root), then the typical arrangement (ninth in soprano above leading-tone), then the best arrangement (root doubled low, ninth clearly above). The wrong voicing sounds muddled; the correct voicing has the characteristic "lush but directed" quality.
ex3 — the critical file for FormaComposition: all three treatments. First: the ninth as appoggiatura — A arrives on the beat, resolves down to G before the chord changes to I (the most classical, most idiomatic). Second: V9 held for three full bars with slow harmonic rhythm (ambient treatment — the ninth just disappears when I arrives). Third: the V9 resolving with the ninth moving as a normal harmonic dissonance. Three completely different textures from the same chord.
ex4 — the full comparison: V7, then V°9 (half-dim), then complete V9 major, then dim7. Hearing all four dominant formations back to back makes the distinctions clear — each has a distinct density, color, and quality of tension.
ex5 — V9 deceptive resolution: V9→I (reference), then V9→VI (surprise), then the continuation that resolves it home. The unexpected VI landing after the full V9 is striking because the dominant ninth had set up such a strong expectation of tonic.
ex6 — secondary V9s: V9ofV approaching the dominant, then V9ofIV approaching the subdominant. Both introduce chromatic notes (F# and Bb respectively) within a C major context.
ex7 — the ambient treatment: a minor V9 held for four full bars with near-silence above it, then dissolving to I without resolution. This is what "slowly moving root progression" with melodic activity above sounds like structurally. The ninth just evaporates — no resolution event, just a change of chord. Load this in Logic and add some melodic motion above the V9 in the piano roll to hear the complete texture.
ex8 — both treatments in one phrase: the first phrase uses the appoggiatura treatment (classical, explicit resolution), the second phrase uses the held V9 (ambient, ninth disappears). The contrast between the two approaches in the same piece is the compositional choice.
