ex1 — nondominant ninth in its three main forms: I9 (D appoggiatura above I, resolves to C); II9 (C appoggiatura above D bass, resolves to B); IV9 with delayed resolution (G ninth above F bass, stays held while harmony changes to II6 beneath it before finally resolving). The delayed version is the most expressive — the harmonic change happens but the melody note lingers.
ex2 — tonic pedal: the low C bass holds while the upper voices move through IV then V7. This is the "V over I" texture — tonic stability below, dominant tension above. In Logic with Solina or pad, this two-bar pattern repeating could anchor an entire section.
ex3 — dominant pedal: the G bass holds while IV (F A C) moves above it. This is the "dominant eleventh" — really just subdominant harmony over a dominant pedal, but the label captures the dissonance profile. The resolution to V→I afterward feels especially earned because of the accumulated tension.
ex4 — double pedal: both C and G held simultaneously in the bass while the upper voices move freely through I, IV, II, V, I. The stability is remarkable — even the chromatic harmonies feel grounded because both key-defining notes are always present. This is the sound Piston identifies as an origin of polytonality.
ex5 — dominant thirteenth: E (the scale's third degree) appearing as an appoggiatura above a V7 chord, then resolving down to D (the fifth of V), then the chord resolves to I. The E→D appoggiatura resolution is the "thirteenth" in action — a sixth above the bass momentarily leaning before settling.
ex6 — the ambient key file: D (a ninth above I) appears in the upper voice and stays there while the harmony cycles I → IV → V7 → I beneath it. D is never explicitly resolved — it just fades with the final release. This is the theoretical justification for the Valhalla Shimmer reverb tail behavior. Piston calls it the "unresolved appoggiatura" — a recognized technique from 1850 onward, not an error.
ex7 — the complete pedal structure: tonic pedal first (IV then V7 above C bass), then dominant pedal (IV above G bass), then standard cadential formula for resolution. This is a usable compositional structure for an entire 16-bar section.
ex8 — full romantic richness: IV9 with delayed resolution, then V7 with thirteenth (E) appoggiatura, resolving to I. Slow, sustained. Load into Logic with Mellotron strings and you'll hear why this style dominated European music from 1850–1910.
