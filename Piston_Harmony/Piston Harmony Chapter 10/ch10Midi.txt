ex1 — the bare skeleton: I–IV–V–I with melody strictly on chord tones. Four clean whole notes. This is what FormaComposition currently generates. Everything else in this chapter is added on top of this.
ex2 — passing tones: first the C→G skip unadorned, then filled in stepwise (C D E F G), then with chromatic passing tones. Hear how the melody goes from an arpeggio to a line.
ex3 — auxiliary tones: upper auxiliary (whole step), lower auxiliary (half step — chromatic, acting as a temporary leading-tone), then the full turn (C→D→C→B→C), then the double auxiliary (D skip to B, back to C).
ex4 — the appoggiatura in four demonstrations: D resolving down to C on I; A resolving down to G on V; B resolving up to C (leading-tone approach); and a full cadential phrase with an appoggiatura at the moment of tonic arrival. The strong-beat landing followed by the weak-beat resolution is the appoggiatura's signature.
ex5 — two suspensions: the 4-3 suspension (C held over from I into V, resolving down to B), and the 7-6 suspension (D held over from V into I, resolving down to C). The clashing dissonance against the new chord is the point — hear it lean into the resolution.
ex6 — échappée and cambiata back to back. The échappée escapes upward before arriving at the lower destination by skip; the cambiata overshoots by a step and returns. Both are brief melodic swerves around a direct path.
ex7 — the anticipation: C5 arriving while V is still held, then becoming the chord tone when I arrives. The forward lean into the resolution.
ex8 — the Piston four-step process heard in sequence: the same 8-chord progression three times. First: pure chord tones (the skeleton). Second: passing tones added between melody notes. Third: an appoggiatura at the final tonic arrival. Same harmony, progressively more expressive surface.
